# Ink Bloom

**Tagline:** Two rival inks fight for the canvas — one drop at a time.

## Premise
Two players drop ink onto a shared white canvas. Ink spreads organically, absorbs weaker ink, and mutates when it touches the enemy. The player who covers more than 60% of the canvas wins. But ink doesn't just spread — it *thinks*.

## Visual Style
- Pure white canvas background
- Rich, saturated ink blobs that pulse and breathe
- Fluid simulation: ink bleeds into nearby cells with soft feathering edges
- Player 1: Deep indigo/violet. Player 2: Burnt amber/crimson
- When inks collide: swirling purple-orange interference patterns
- Victory: the winning ink floods the screen in a gorgeous bloom burst

## Core Mechanics
- Turn-based: each player places 1 drop per turn
- Drops spread 1–3 cells per turn based on "saturation" (how surrounded they are)
- **Absorb:** Your ink touching enemy ink with 2:1 ratio converts it
- **Mutation:** When inks collide in equal strength, a neutral grey zone forms — a "dead pixel" that blocks both
- **Bloom Move:** Once per game, spend 3 turns to trigger a mega-spread from your largest cluster

## What Makes It Weird
The board is a 16x16 grid of "fiber cells" — each cell has a random absorbency rating that affects spread speed. Some cells are "wax resistant" and never get absorbed. The board itself is alive; every 5 turns a random cell becomes a "bloom vent" that accelerates whatever ink touches it first.

The result is a game that looks like painting but plays like territorial chess — deeply visual, utterly unpredictable.
