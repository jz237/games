Concept Overview

Neon Tinkerer is a single-player HTML5 top-down puzzle-action game set in a neon-lit cyberworkshop. The player is a tinkerer who repairs and upgrades small robotic companions by solving spatial and logic puzzles, gathering parts, and surviving short reactive hazards. The core loop blends light action (dodging hazards, timing) with inventory-based puzzle solving (placing gears, routing power), giving approximately 30–90 minutes of play across a progression of workshops.

Player Goals

- Repair 2 base-model companions (playable 'levels') to demonstrate progression.
- Learn new components and mechanics (power routing, gear alignment, timing circuits).
- Earn upgrades that unlock a final multi-step repair challenge.

Core Mechanics

1. Movement: 8-directional top-down movement.
2. Parts collection: Break crates or solve mini-puzzles to collect parts.
3. Assembly puzzles: Place parts on a grid/board to complete circuits/gears.
4. Upgrades: Simple one-slot upgrades modify speed, carry capacity, or tool abilities.
5. Short hazards: Timed sparks or spinning blades that require timing to avoid.
