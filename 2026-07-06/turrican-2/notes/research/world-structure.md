# World & Stage Structure — Research Notes

> Original homage tribute. No ROM data, no ripped assets. These notes capture
> facts about the ORIGINAL Amiga game to guide an original re-creation.

## Authoritative structure (verified)

**Turrican II: The Final Fight** (Amiga, Factor 5 / Rainbow Arts, 1991).
Run-and-gun exploration platformer. **5 worlds / 11 stages total**, distributed
**2 / 2 / 3 / 2 / 2**. Four worlds (1, 2, 4, 5) are large multi-directional
maze-like EXPLORE platform stages; World 3 is a 3-stage horizontal-scrolling
SHOOT-'EM-UP detour (Bren pilots a spaceship). The game ends with the fight
against the final boss, **"The Machine."**

| World | Name | Type | Stages | Theme |
|-------|------|------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Desert / Landorin Surface | platform | 1-1, 1-2 | Outdoor rocky desert surface of planet Landorin; open sky, powerful winds |
| 2 | Submerged Dungeon | platform | 2-1, 2-2 | Underground/underwater caverns; submerge into an underground lake with all-directional movement; then a vertical wind-climb |
| 3 | Corridor | shmup | 3-1, 3-2, 3-3 | Space/mechanical tunnel shoot-'em-up on rails; Gradius-style speed-up |
| 4 | Walker Factory | platform | 4-1, 4-2 | Industrial factory/machine interior; fire-breathing Walker statues; PENULTIMATE world |
| 5 | Alien Ship | platform | 5-1, 5-2 | H.R. Giger-inspired biomechanical alien world; FINAL world, ends vs "The Machine" |

### Per-stage detail

- **1-1** platform — Rocky/desert surface, open sky; introductory multi-directional maze; introduces morph-ball wheel, laser/spread weapons, rotating power-line beam.
- **1-2** platform — Desert continues into deeper terrain with waterfalls complicating vertical movement.
- **2-1** platform — Open area into an underwater cavern/lake; free-directional underwater movement. Very enemy-dense (original ran at reduced frame rate / less parallax here).
- **2-2** platform — Linear cavern path (with optional bonus detours) ending in a vertical wind-climb area. Transition to World 3 (steals a spaceship).
- **3-1** shmup — Horizontal-scrolling blast-'em-up (simple).
- **3-2** shmup — Horizontal shooter that mixes in vertical scrolling (mixed-axis).
- **3-3** shmup — High-velocity reaction-test finale; fastest of the three. (One map source labels it vertical; reviews call it the fastest.)
- **4-1** platform — Labyrinthine factory interior; fire-breathing Walker statues; conveyor belts, spinning nuts on bolts, crushing machinery.
- **4-2** platform — Deeper factory/machine complex leading toward the final world; contains a machine boss.
- **5-1** platform — Biomechanical/organic alien environment; C64-wiki notes it "reminds one of the Alien movies." Rooftop-train-style set piece (trains of bone/flesh — fan lore, unverified).
- **5-2** platform — Biomechanical finale; per Kyzer a vertical-climb "Finale" with no standard map; culminates in "The Machine" and the ending.

## Corrections applied from verification pass

1. **Stage 2-3 does NOT exist** — it was fabricated in the raw claim. The Fandom
   "Submerged Dungeon" page states World 2 has exactly two areas; Kyzer's map
   archive shows only 2.1 and 2.2. Total = **11 stages (2/2/3/2/2)**, World 3
   is the only 3-stage world.
2. The "12 levels (Worlds 2 AND 3 have 3)" figure comes only from an old
   Zzap!64 review quoted by amigareviews — an outlier, not corroborated.
   Rejected.
3. **World ordering is uncontested**: W4 = Walker Factory (penultimate),
   W5 = Alien Ship (final). HG101 does NOT actually order factory vs alien;
   no real conflict. Fandom + Kyzer + C64-wiki confirm this order.
4. World 5 is "Alien Ship" (not "Alien Labyrinth" — that's the ORIGINAL
   Turrican, different game). Do not conflate.
5. On Amiga, **5-2 is an on-foot platform finale**, not a shmup. The
   "final level is a shmup" fact belongs to the CONSOLE ports (Super Turrican
   lineage), not the Amiga original.

## Other facts

- Levels are huge — whole game often cited at ~1500 screens.
- Each stage has a **countdown timer**; expiry costs a life. Exact seconds not
  authoritatively confirmed.
- Every stage hides secret rooms / power-ups. (Specific set pieces such as the
  "1-2 flying-saucer worth ~2 extra lives," "2-2 grab-boss death barrier,"
  "steel dragon," "big mech," "rooftop bone-train" are plausible fan lore —
  NOT independently confirmed. Treat as flavor, not canon.)
- Lives system with collectible diamonds/extra lives; password/continue
  mechanics exist. Exact starting lives / continues not pinned to a primary source.
- Boss-per-stage names are largely undocumented ("mega-monsters"); only the
  final boss "The Machine" is firmly attested.
